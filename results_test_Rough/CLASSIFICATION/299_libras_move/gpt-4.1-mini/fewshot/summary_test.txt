### Summary
The AutoML process began with thorough data profiling confirming an entirely numeric dataset dominated by geospatial coordinates. A manual grammar specifying possible pipeline components (imputers, encoders, scalers, selectors, classifiers) guided pipeline synthesis. The core search strategy deployed multiple Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) simulations to explore combinations. Pipelines mostly leveraged MaxAbsScaler for feature scaling and GenericUnivariateSelect for feature selection. Diverse classifiers were tested, with ensemble and kernel methods among top candidates, resulting in progressive improvements in evaluation scores. After several simulations and a time-limited search, the process terminated having generated 30 candidate pipelines for further tuning or deployment.
