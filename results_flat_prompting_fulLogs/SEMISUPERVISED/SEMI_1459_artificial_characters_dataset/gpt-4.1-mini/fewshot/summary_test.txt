 AutoML Process Summarization

### Data Profiling Phase
- Columns: 8
- Column types:
  - Columns 0 to 5 (d3mIndex, V1, V2, V3, V4, V5): Integer
  - Columns 6 and 7 (V6, V7): Float
- No non-numeric features detected.
- No missing values in the dataset.
- Dataset was large (6045 instances) so it was downsampled to 2000 for faster processing.

### Pipeline Search and Synthesis Phase

- The task is identified as a SEMISUPERVISED_TASK.
- A manual grammar was created defining pipeline structure, including:
  - IMPUTER (missing value handling)
  - ENCODERS (categorical encoding, here 'E' meaning empty/no encoding)
  - FEATURE_SCALER (various scalers and 'E' for no scaling)
  - SEMISUPERVISED_CLASSIFIER (semisupervised learning components)
  - CLASSIFIER (supervised classifiers)
  - LABELPROPAGATION_CLASSIFIER (label propagation techniques)
- Multiple pipeline structures with various combinations of these components were explored.

#### Iteration 1: Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS)-driven Pipeline Exploration

- Many MCTS simulations were performed, each representing a trial pipeline construction and evaluation.
- Pipelines were progressively built by replacing grammar symbols with specific components:
  - IMPUTER was mostly replaced by 'E' meaning no imputation needed.
  - ENCODERS mostly replaced by 'E', no encoding applied (likely no categorical features).
  - FEATURE_SCALER experimented with MaxAbsScaler, StandardScaler, RobustScaler, and sometimes removed.
  - SEMISUPERVISED_CLASSIFIER used primitives including AutonBox, SelfTrainingClassifier.
  - LABELPROPAGATION_CLASSIFIER included SkLabelPropagation, SkLabelSpreading primitives.
  - CLASSIFIER varied among multiple sklearn classifiers like BernoulliNB, LinearSVC, KNeighborsClassifier, SVC, etc.

#### Pipeline Evaluation and Challenges

- Pipelines were scored using cross-validation, with the MCTS predicting pipeline performance scores.
- An error occurred with pipelines including AutonBox where a pandas DataFrame was used with a `toarray()` method not supported — indicating a transform step expecting sparse matrices but got dense DataFrame.
- Another error occurred when a SelfTrainingClassifier was combined with LinearSVC, which lacks a `predict_proba` method, causing the scoring to fail (since SelfTrainingClassifier requires base estimators that can output probability estimates).

#### Resolution and Final Pipelines

- The search continued after removing or replacing problematic steps (like replacing SelfTrainingClassifier or AutonBox components, or removing FEATURE_SCALER).
- The best pipelines used RobustScaler or StandardScaler combined with SkLabelSpreading as the semisupervised classifier.
- The highest recorded pipeline score was approximately 0.6865.
- The search was terminated due to reaching the time limit.
- One successful final pipeline was:
  - Step 1: Scaling with StandardScaler or RobustScaler
  - Step 2: Semisupervised classification with SkLabelSpreading

### Summary

- The AutoML system began by profiling a dataset of 8 mostly numeric columns and no missing values.
- Due to data size, downsampling was applied.
- A grammar-based search with MCTS was conducted to assemble pipelines fitting a semisupervised learning task.
- Various preprocessors and semisupervised classifiers were tested alongside classical supervised classifiers.
- Errors in pipeline evaluation were encountered with incompatible components, fixed by adapting pipeline elements.
- The search concluded with one well-performing pipeline involving scaling and the SkLabelSpreading semisupervised classifier.
- Final pipeline scoring reached ~0.6865 before search terminated on timeout.
